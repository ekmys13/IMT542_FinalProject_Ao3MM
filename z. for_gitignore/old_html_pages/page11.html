    Summary

    Every building in Newfaire has its ghost stories, stories that a much younger Howard Margrove hadn’t given much mind to when he had been a student at Briarbank College. He had been there to learn, to study, not to gossip with those less academically minded then he himself was. Still, he couldn’t help but overhear the tales told in the hallways or, later, in the faculty lounge, an indignity Howard had suffered for as only as long as it had taken to install a gas burner in his office so that he could make his own tea without having to rub elbows with the rest of the faculty.

    At the time, Howard had thought the stories nothing more than the simple imaginings of overworked or inebriated minds. Yes, he had occasionally seen blue lights out of the corner of his eye that had vanished when faced fully, but that could be explained away by the flickering gaslight or the inconsistent flaring of electric bulbs. Howard believed in tricks of the light more than he did spirits and ghosts.

    That had been Before, of course. Before he had joined Candela Obscura.

    After that, well, anything had seemed more possible than it had before.
